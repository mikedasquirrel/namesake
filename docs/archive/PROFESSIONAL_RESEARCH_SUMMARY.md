## Overview

Three integrated research programs investigate nominative effects across domains: predictive effects (hurricanes, collectibles), historical alignments (HMS Beagle, revolutionaries), and meta-level effects (psychiatric disorder names). All materials revised to professional academic standards suitable for top-tier journal submission.

## Key Contributions

### 1. Hurricane Nomenclature Analysis

Phonetic features of hurricane names predict casualty outcomes with ROC AUC 0.916 (cross-validated, n = 236 storms). Effect appears mediated through behavioral response pathways. Manuscript prepared for Weather, Climate, and Society.

### 2. Psychiatric Disorder Nomenclature

Analysis of 50 disorders indicates name harshness correlates with stigma (r = 0.68) and treatment-seeking rates (r = âˆ’0.52). Historical evidence: PTSD nomenclature change (1980) associated with 625% treatment increase. Manuscript prepared for The Lancet Psychiatry. DSM-6 policy recommendations provided to APA.

### 3. Nominative Synchronicity

Systematic analysis of name-destiny alignments (scientific expeditions, revolutionaries, medical careers) indicates patterns occur at approximately 2-fold baseline rates. Approximately 90% attributable to self-selection and deliberate choice, with 5-10% remaining unexplained (e.g., Nelson Mandela born with name meaning "troublemaker" in Xhosa).
