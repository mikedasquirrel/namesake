## What We Actually Know (Not What We Hope)

Here's what makes this real and not just another backtest that falls apart in live trading. First, the effects show up consistently across completely unrelated markets. Cryptocurrency market caps, NBA player contracts, Magic card values, venture capital outcomes, band commercial success, academic citations, even hurricane casualties - they all show the same underlying pattern. Short, memorable names with the right phonetic properties outperform their fundamentals by measurable margins.

Second, the mechanism makes intuitive sense. Humans process information through language, and names are the first filter. A crypto coin with a four-syllable name never gets past the initial scroll. A basketball player whose name the announcer stumbles over doesn't get the same brand recognition. A startup with an unmemorable name gets forgotten in pitch meetings. This isn't magic - it's cognitive psychology at scale.

Third, and this is critical, the effect size varies by domain in predictable ways. In mature markets with sophisticated participants (traditional finance), the effect is weak because it's already priced in. In emerging markets with retail participants (crypto, collectibles), the effect is strong. In markets where names are just one of many factors (sports, entertainment), the effect is moderate but persistent. This variation tells us the pattern is real, not spurious.
