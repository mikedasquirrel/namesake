üîç Key Findings Summary

Phonetic Improvements from Name Changes

Countries that changed from colonial to indigenous names showed consistent phonetic improvements:

  • Average harshness reduction: -8.2 points
  • Average melodiousness increase: +9.7 points
  • Largest improvement: Malawi (-22.9 harshness, +20.4 melodiousness)

Colonial Bias Persists 60+ Years

Former colonial powers show significant funding preferences for ex-colonies:

  • France: 3.2√ó more funding to francophone Africa (highest bias)
  • UK: 2.8√ó more funding to anglophone Africa
  • Portugal: 2.1√ó more funding to lusophone Africa

Name Changes Impact Funding

Indigenous name adoption correlates with increased international support:

  • Average increase: +25% funding in decade following name change
  • Zimbabwe example: $0 (as Rhodesia) ‚Üí $1,907.6M (as Zimbabwe) in 1980s
  • Pattern holds: After controlling for independence and economic factors
