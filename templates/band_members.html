Research Focus

Novel Angle: While our Band Names analysis examines band nomenclature (e.g., "The Beatles", "Metallica"), this study analyzes individual band member names and their correlation with musical roles.

Research Questions

  1. Do phonetic features predict which role (bassist/drummer/vocalist/etc.) a member plays?
  2. Does name 'harshness' correlate with drummer/guitarist roles?
  3. Do 'smoother' names correlate with vocalist/songwriter roles?
  4. Does collective member name composition predict band commercial success?
  5. Are there temporal shifts in name-role patterns?

Hypotheses

H1: Phonetic Harshness → Drummer/Guitarist

Expectation: Harsher names (plosives, fricatives) predict rhythm section roles.

Analogous to NFL linemen analysis.

H2: Phonetic Smoothness → Vocalist

Expectation: Smoother names (liquids, glides) predict lead vocalist roles.

Expected effect: r = 0.25-0.35

H3: Syllable Patterns by Role

Expectation: Shorter names (1-2 syllables) for rhythm section (bass/drums).

Longer names for frontmen/vocalists.

H4: Collective Composition → Band Success

Expectation: Phonetically diverse member names predict greater commercial success.

"Harsh bassist + smooth vocalist" = optimal combination
