Research Questions
  1. Do professional sports rosters reflect American demographic name distributions, or do they deviate significantly?
  2. How does roster "americanness" (phonetic patterns) vary by sport and team?
  3. What is the melodiousness of each roster relative to its sport's characteristics?
  4. How do teams compare to their league averages and to each other in terms of name composition?
  5. Do different sports attract different demographic name patterns?

Sport-Level Comparisons

Americanness by Sport

Americanness score (0-100) measures phonetic patterns typical of Anglo-American names. Higher scores indicate more traditional American naming patterns.

Melodiousness by Sport

Melodiousness score (0-100) measures phonetic flow and harmony. Sport-adjusted scores account for contact level, precision, and action speed.

Methodology

Americanness Score

Calculated from:

  • Anglo phonetic patterns (40%)
  • International markers (30%)
  • Syllable structure (20%)
  • Name origin classification (10%)

Melodiousness Score

Calculated from:

  • Phonetic flow (30%)
  • Vowel harmony (25%)
  • Syllabic rhythm (25%)
  • Harshness inverse (20%)
Sport-adjusted based on contact level, precision, and action speed.

Demographic Classification

Names classified into:

  • Anglo (traditional American)
  • Latino (Spanish/Latin American)
  • Asian (East/South/Southeast Asian)
  • Black (African American patterns)
  • Other (mixed/unclassified)
