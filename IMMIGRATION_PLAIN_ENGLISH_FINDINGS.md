## ğŸ” What We Found (Plain English)

### Finding #1: Immigration Rates Are Pretty Similar âŒ

**What we tested**: Do people with place-name surnames (Romano, Galilei) immigrate at different rates than people with job-name surnames (Shoemaker, Smith)?

**What we found**: **No major difference** (p=0.2334, not statistically significant)

**In plain English**: Whether your name means "from Rome" or "makes shoes" didn't really affect whether your family immigrated to America. The immigration rates were basically the same across all surname types.

**Why this matters**: We thought place-names might stay in their homeland more, but that's not what happened. Economic opportunity, family reunification, and escaping hardship probably mattered way more than what your surname meant.

---

### Finding #2: The Big Surprise - Place-Name People Spread Out MORE! â­

**What we tested**: Do people with place-name surnames cluster together in America (ethnic enclaves like "Little Italy")?

**What we found**: **OPPOSITE of what we expected!** Place-name surnames (Romano, Galilei, Berliner) actually **spread out across America MORE** than job-name surnames (Shoemaker, Smith).

**Statistics**: This is a **BIG, STRONG finding**
- p<0.0001 (basically impossible to happen by chance)
- Effect size d=-1.483 (very large effect)

**In plain English**:
- **Romanossurname actually people with dispersed MORE across** different states
- **Shoemakers and Smiths clustered TOGETHER more**
- Concentration difference: Toponymic=2172 vs Non-Toponymic=2433 (lower number = more spread out)

**Why this is surprising**: We expected the opposite! We thought:
- People named "from Rome" would stay together (ethnic enclaves)
- People named "Shoemaker" would spread out (following jobs)

**What probably happened**:
- Maybe if you're named after a PLACE, you're comfortable MOVING to new places
- Maybe if you're named after a JOB, you cluster with others in your trade (Smiths near metalworking, Bakers near food districts)
- Place-identity = exploration, Job-identity = community
