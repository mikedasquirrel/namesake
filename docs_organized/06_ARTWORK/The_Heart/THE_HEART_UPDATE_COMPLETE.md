# The Heart: Major Conceptual Update Complete

## From Nail to Heart

The core concept has evolved from a nail/cross to a broken heart. This is more elegant, more universal, more accurate to the experience.

---

## What Changed

### Concept Transformation

**Before:** The nail that pins fluid consciousness into fixed identity. The cross. The instrument of crucifixion.

**Now:** The heart that's whole when seen as whole, broken when seen from the angle of brokenness. The fracture line. The cost of being made definite.

### Why This Is Better

1. **More universal** - Everyone understands a broken heart
2. **More accurate** - Bipolarity is cycles of breaking and reforming, not crucifixion
3. **More ambiguous** - Heart could be whole or broken (both valid interpretations)
4. **More magical** - Hearts are mystical, romantic, folk-art friendly
5. **Less explicitly Christian** - Works across traditions while still profound
6. **More about dissolution** - Breaking and scattering, not pinning

### The Visual Transformation

**At 0° under full moon:**
- Complete heart shape
- Glowing with soft pink/red light
- Whole, intact, beautiful
- Love and wholeness from mathematical patterns
- Stars scattered, mystical atmosphere

**Transition (15° - 28°):**
- Heart begins showing stress
- Fracture line appearing down center
- Moon waning from full to crescent

**At 32.7° under crescent moon:**
- Heart split in two
- Halves separating (35px apart)
- Dashed fracture line down center
- Fragments scattering from the break (8 pieces)
- Representative names circling the broken center
- Same data, but revealing fragmentation instead of wholeness

- Witnesses the breaking
- Partial light reveals what full light hides
- Waning as wholeness gives way to fragmentation
- The cosmic cycle mirroring the psychological cycle

## Why This Works Better

**The nail concept:**
- Too explicitly violent
- Crucifixion metaphor heavy-handed
- "BIPOLAR" label too personal/direct
- Cross shape too overtly Christian

**The broken heart concept:**
- Universal (everyone knows heartbreak)
- Ambiguous (could be whole or broken - both valid)
- Personal without being specific
- Works across spiritual traditions
- About cycles (breaking/reforming) not permanent damage
- More accurate to bipolarity experience
- More beautiful visually
- More tragic/poignant than violent
