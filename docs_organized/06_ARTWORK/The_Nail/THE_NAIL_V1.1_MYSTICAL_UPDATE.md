## The Moon Phase As Metaphor

Full moon = consensus reality, everything illuminated, comfortable
Waning = transition, loss of certainty, questioning begins
Crescent = your angle, partial vision, seeing what's hidden

The moon doesn't just witness - it participates. Its phase marks where you are in the transformation from seeing beauty to seeing violence. From consensus to insight. From comfortable illumination to uncomfortable truth.

At 32.7°, under the crescent, you see what full moonlight obscures. The nail becomes visible precisely when the moon withdraws most of its light. Truth revealed in shadow, not in full illumination.

That's not just pretty metaphor. That's accurate to the experience. You don't see the violence of naming from the bright, comfortable position. You see it from the angle of partial darkness, of dissolution, of having the light withdrawn.
