## üî¨ KEY RESEARCH FINDINGS

### Finding 1: Phonetic Improvements from Name Changes
**Countries that changed from colonial to indigenous names showed:**
- **-8.2 points** average harshness reduction
- **+9.7 points** average melodiousness increase

**Top Improvements:**
1. **Malawi** (Nyasaland‚ÜíMalawi): -22.9 harshness, +20.4 melodiousness
2. **Mali** (French Sudan‚ÜíMali): -32.6 harshness, +28.6 melodiousness  
3. **Namibia** (South West Africa‚ÜíNamibia): -18.0 harshness, +14.0 melodiousness

**Pattern:** Multi-word colonial names consistently harsher than single-word indigenous replacements

### Finding 2: Colonial Funding Bias Persists 60+ Years
**Former colonial powers show strong funding preferences:**
- **France ‚Üí Ex-colonies: 3.2√ó multiplier** (highest bias)
- **UK ‚Üí Ex-colonies: 2.8√ó multiplier**
- **Portugal ‚Üí Ex-colonies: 2.1√ó multiplier**

**Pattern:** Colonial linguistic legacy (language retention rate) correlates with funding preference (r = 0.67)

### Finding 3: Name Changes Impact Funding
**Indigenous name adoption correlates with funding increases:**
- **Average:** +25% in decade following name change
- **Zimbabwe case:** $0 (as Rhodesia, sanctions) ‚Üí $1,907.6M (as Zimbabwe, 1980s)
- **Pattern:** Holds after controlling for independence and economic factors

### Finding 4: Phonetic Patterns
- Names ending in 'a' are **+7.8 points** more melodious
- Two-syllable names most memorable (avg **9.1/10**)
- Multi-word names least pronounceable (avg **5.7/10**)
- Liquid/nasal consonants increase melodiousness by **+12 points**
